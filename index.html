About

Based in Mishawaka, Indiana, Schurz Communications, Inc. is a diversified privately-owned, nationwide communications company. The company publishes eleven daily and eight weekly newspapers in medium and small markets with a combined circulation of nearly 225,000. It also has ten television stations, owns thirteen radio stations and operates three others, owns four cable companies, and a phone directory. For more information, visit Schurz online at www.schurz.com.

Why should I sign up?

The Challenge offers a unique opportunity for you to test your creativity and skills in online and mobile media technology. South Bend Code School and IPND is available as mentoring resources throughout the competition. They also will be providing coding grants for select teams without coding resources of their own to advance prototype development. Through the challenge, any Notre Dame student, on their own or with a team, will have the chance to work with online media technology, create innovative ideas in the area of media technology, and work on developing and creating a prototype of the idea with the guidance of experienced mentors, including Challenge partner South Bend Code School.

2016 Winner

The first place team, Covur, won the top prize of $5,000 for its unique platform that helps local businesses market directly to their audiences by automating email list growth using WiFi. To find out more about last year’s first prize winner, visit their website https://covur.co/. Other prizes were awarded to Commentate, a service that allows sports fans customize the sportscaster they listen to during live events, Let It Out!, a mental wellness app that allows individuals to record and regulate their day-to- day emotions and two prizes were given for the best coding to Jamocracy, which enables users to add songs to Spotify playlists via text message, and SquadSnap, which brings ephemeral pictures and videos to group communications.

Judging Criteria

Judging should focus on cohesiveness of the concept and prototype, with potential for impact and degree of innovation versus the existing landscape considered once cohesiveness thresholds are met. There is a prize for the best coding, which is distinct from (and potentially in addition to) the overall prize. To be eligible for the coding prize, the coding needs to have been completed by current Notre Dame students who are members of the team. Coding prize eligibility will be pre screened and eligible teams will be indicated on the judging form which will be distributed at the competition.

Final Idea Overview

Questions to Consider

  • What pain/unmet need are you addressing?
  • What’s your amazing solution?
  • Who are your customers? How will this change their experience?
  • How might customers buy/acquire your solution?
  • What experiences have your team brought to the development of this idea?
  • What have you done so far that proves that you know your customers?
  • What are the next most important things needed?
